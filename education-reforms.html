Education Reforms

Description

Education in America used to be for the rich and the elite. However, the Education Reform movement helped give all kids access to education and helped get kids out of child labor situations.

Dates of Activity

The Education Reform movement started in the early 1800s when reformers agreed that education should be available and free to everyone in the U.S. The movement ended state by state as each state began building common schools and passing laws requiring kids to go to school. The last state to make it mandatory for children to attend school was Mississippi in 1917.

Important Leaders

Horace Mann was an activist for public education. He was appointed Secretary of the Massachusetts Board of Education in 1837. His philosophy was that for a democratic society to function, its citizens must be educated. He advocated for government-funded schools that children were required to attend. He is known as the father of American education for efforts.

Reforms Sought Out

Education reformers wanted education to be available to everyone, so they started to push for places like public schools to be opened. Many reformers wanted schools to be funded by the government. Other reformers also wanted it to be mandatory by law for kids to attend school.

Significant Dates

  • 1837 - Horace Mann is appointed the Secretary of the Massachusetts Board of Education and begins campaigning for common, government schools.
  • 1852 - Massachusetts is the first state to make it mandatory for children to attend school.
  • 1853 - Every northern state has a common school.
  • 1917 - Mississippi is the last state to make common school mandatory for kids.

Legislation Changes

While there is no national law requiring children to attend school, every state in the U.S. requires children to go to school. Every child in every state has access to public education or private education if their parents wish. However, one of these is required.

Summary and Impact

The Education Reform Movement was massively important when it came to getting kids out of factories and providing them with a solid education. Thanks to reformers like Horace Mann, generations now can be educated to become useful to society and contribute to America’s democracy.
